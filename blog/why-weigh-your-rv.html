Why RV Owners Should Periodically Weigh Their RV

If you’ve ever wondered whether weighing your RV really matters, you’re not alone.

Many RV owners focus on staying under published weight limits, but how that weight is distributed — and how it changes over time — is just as important.

Periodic weighing helps RV owners understand how their rig is actually loaded for travel, identify potential safety concerns, and make informed decisions about cargo, tire pressure, and overall setup.

What Does It Mean to Weigh Your RV?

Weighing your RV means measuring the total weight of your fully loaded rig — including cargo, water, fuel, and passengers — and comparing it to manufacturer weight ratings such as GVWR and GAWR.

You can learn more about these terms on our RV Definitions page.

Why RV Owners Should Weigh Periodically

Loading Changes Over Time

As you travel, it’s easy to accumulate gear, tools, and supplies. Even small additions can change how weight is distributed throughout your RV.

State Weight Laws Still Apply

Even though RVs are recreational vehicles, they are still subject to state weight laws. Exceeding weight limits can result in fines, restricted travel, or required load adjustments.

Safety Considerations

An overloaded RV can impact braking, handling, and stability. Understanding your weight helps reduce these risks.

Tire Longevity

Tires are designed to carry specific loads. When overloaded, they generate excess heat, which can lead to premature wear or failure.

Why Four-Corner Weighing Provides the Most Complete Picture

Traditional truck scales measure axle weights but assume weight is evenly distributed from side to side.

RVs are rarely balanced due to slide-outs, kitchens, and storage layouts. This means one side — or even one tire — can carry significantly more weight than another.

Four-corner weighing measures each individual wheel position, providing a much more accurate picture of how your RV is loaded.

Benefits of Four-Corner Weighing

  • Identifies side-to-side imbalance
  • Reveals overloaded tires
  • Improves tire pressure accuracy
  • Helps with cargo adjustments

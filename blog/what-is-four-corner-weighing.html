What Is Four-Corner Weighing?

If you’re new to RVing — or even if you’ve been on the road for a while — you’ve probably heard people throw around terms like four-corner weighing, axle weights, and CAT scales.

Weight is one of the most misunderstood topics in the RV world, not because RV owners don’t care, but because the information is often fragmented, oversimplified, or explained in a way that assumes a trucking background.

What Is Four-Corner Weighing?

Four-corner weighing is the process of measuring the weight carried by each individual tire position on your RV. Not just the total weight, and not just axle weights — but each corner.

Photo placeholder: RV positioned on individual wheel scales, each tire weighed separately

Why Four-Corner Weighing Matters

RVs are homes on wheels. Slide-outs, appliances, holding tanks, and floorplans all influence weight distribution, often unevenly. An RV can be under its published weight ratings and still have an overloaded tire.

Photo placeholder: Diagram showing uneven side-to-side weight distribution

Why CAT Scales Work for Truckers — But Not Always for RVers

CAT scales are excellent tools and are widely used in the commercial trucking industry. However, they measure combined axle weights and do not show how weight is distributed side-to-side.

Photo placeholder: CAT scale platform compared to individual wheel scales

How Four-Corner Weighing Works

Each tire is weighed individually, allowing owners to understand true tire loads, set proper tire pressures, and identify imbalances that axle-only scales cannot reveal.

Photo placeholder: Technician recording scale readings beside an RV

Four-corner weighing replaces guesswork with information, helping RV owners travel with confidence and clarity.
